# Blacklisting Procedure

## Overview

Blacklisting represents the action of restricting a user from assuming certain roles. This measure is rarely implemented and requires serious consideration from either staff or the player community.

## Eligibility Criteria

A user becomes a candidate for blacklisting only upon demonstrating severe incompetence or clear inability to perform their assigned role. This determination must be evident to the administrative team or broader player base.

## Purpose

The primary objective involves preventing users from repeatedly committing identical mistakes that negatively impact overall gameplay experience and community health.

## Three Blacklist Categories

### 1. Timeout

**Application**: Any Admin or Game Master can initiate a timeout motion, subject to discussion and voting among administrative staff.

**Duration & Removal**: Administrative consensus determines the timeout duration. Once expired, the individual regains role access.

**Example Scenario**: A player joins while under substance influence, assumes a critical leadership position, and fails to execute responsibilities effectively.

### 2. Barring

**Application**: Any Admin or Game Master can propose barring, requiring administrative discussion and voting.

**Lifting Restrictions**: The barred individual must arrange an offline demonstration session with an Admin or GM, showcasing corrected performance. Alternatively, a conversation acknowledging mistakes and demonstrating proper techniques may suffice.

**Usage**: Implements when incompetence appears permanent rather than temporary.

### 3. Temporary Barring

**Application**: An Admin or GM may issue immediate temporary barring for gameplay preservation purposes.

**Duration**: Lasts only the current day.

**Usage**: Functions as an emergency intervention for problematic behavior, including excessive AFK periods or unresponsiveness, typically preceding formal blacklisting procedures.

**Consequences**: Continued resistance escalates the situation appropriately.
